Project Description

For a HostPenny infrastructure initiative, I conducted a financial feasibility and cost-benefit analysis to evaluate transitioning from a physical server environment to a virtualized infrastructure. The existing environment consisted of 25,000 physical servers across four groups supporting mission-critical and non-mission-critical applications and backups. Utilization rates were low, while power consumption and operational overhead were high, making the current environment cost-prohibitive.

Background

The physical server environment generated significant ongoing costs from maintenance, power usage, and administrative labor. To support an informed decision, I analyzed both the current and proposed environments using 5-year projections and categorized expenses into hard costs (hardware, power, maintenance) and soft costs (server administration and backup).

Findings

  • Current physical environment (5-year total): $4,087,238,507.34
  • Proposed virtualized environment (5-year total): $3,377,202,492.93
  • The proposed virtualized environment included an initial investment of $120.47 million in new servers and software, plus ongoing operational costs.

Results and Conclusion

The analysis showed that proceeding with virtualization would generate approximately $710 million in savings over five years, representing a 17.4% reduction in overall infrastructure costs. The primary savings came from reduced power usage and lower server administration costs due to improved utilization of virtual servers. Overall, the long-term benefits outweighed the initial investment.

Recommendation

Based on the projected cost savings and efficiency improvements, I recommended that HostPenny proceed with the virtualization project. This initiative reduces operational expenses, streamlines server management, and supports sustainable growth over the next five years.
